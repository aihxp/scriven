Structure the guide as a linear walkthrough with these sections:

**1. Prerequisites**
- An AI coding agent (Claude Code, Cursor, Gemini CLI, or any of the 9 supported runtimes)
- Node.js 20+ (for the installer)
- That's it — no other dependencies for the core workflow

**2. Install Scriven** (Step 1)
- `npx scriven@latest`
- Brief explanation: this installs slash commands into your agent
- Mention it auto-detects your platform

**3. Try the Demo (Optional)** (Step 2)
- `/scr:demo` — explore a pre-built watchmaker story
- This is a "try before you commit" step — see what a complete project looks like
- Mention what's in the demo: 4 drafted scenes, voice profile, editor notes

**4. Start Your Project** (Step 3)
- `/scr:new-work` — creates a new writing project
- Explain: asks only 3 questions (progressive disclosure), creates context files
- Show what files get created (OUTLINE.md, STYLE-GUIDE.md, etc.)

**5. Develop Your Story** (Step 4)
- `/scr:discuss` — brainstorm and refine your ideas with the AI
- Explain: this is where you shape your story before drafting

**6. Write Your First Draft** (Step 5)
- `/scr:draft` — draft your first unit (scene, chapter section, etc.)
- Explain: the drafter loads your Voice DNA and writes in your voice
- Mention: each unit is drafted in a fresh context for consistency

**7. What's Next**
- `/scr:next` — always knows the next step
- Brief mention of other workflows: editing (/scr:editor-review), publishing (/scr:publish), etc.
- Link to docs/command-reference.md for the full command list

Keep each step short — 3-5 sentences plus a code block. The whole guide should be readable in 5 minutes and executable in 10.
