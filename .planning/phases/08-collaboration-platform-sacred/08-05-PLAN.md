**Sacred translation rules:**
- **Formal equivalence:** Word-for-word as much as possible. Preserve source language syntax. Footnote idiomatic expressions. Academic audiences. Prioritize precision over readability.
- **Dynamic equivalence:** Thought-for-thought. Natural target language expression. Preserve meaning, not form. General audiences. Prioritize clarity.
- **Paraphrase:** Free rendering focusing on modern accessibility. Simplify complex theology. Conversational tone. New readers. Prioritize engagement.
- **Interlinear:** Source word, transliteration, gloss, target word for each element. Scholarly tool, not readable prose.

**Canonical alignment:** When set, match vocabulary and phrasing of the specified canonical translation where the same passages are being rendered. E.g., if canonical_alignment is "kjv", use "lovingkindness" not "steadfast love" for hesed. If "nrsv", use gender-inclusive language.

**Preserve source terms:** Terms in `preserve_source_terms` array are NEVER translated. They appear in the source language (with transliteration if non-Latin script) and are footnoted on first occurrence. Example: "YHWH", "hesed", "shalom", "dharma", "sutra".

**Liturgical preservation:** When true, preserve the rhythmic and musical qualities of liturgical passages. Prioritize how the text sounds when read aloud or chanted. Maintain parallelism, meter, and cadence even at the cost of literal accuracy.
